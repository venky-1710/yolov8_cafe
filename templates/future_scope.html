⚠️ Known Limitations
🌑 Low-Light Performance High Impact

YOLOv8n struggles significantly in poorly lit environments. Detection accuracy can drop below 60% in dimly lit cafes or at night with minimal artificial lighting.

Mitigation Use infrared-capable cameras or increase ambient lighting around monitored zones.
👤 Occlusion & Overlapping High Impact

When people stand very close together or partially obscure each other, the model may under-count. This is inherent to single-camera, 2D detection.

Mitigation Deploy multiple camera angles and merge detection results for improved coverage.
💻 CPU-Only Inference Speed Medium Impact

On CPU, inference is limited to ~10 FPS at 320px resolution. Processing higher-resolution video or deeper model variants will introduce noticeable lag.

Mitigation Frame-skipping is already applied. A GPU upgrade or ONNX export can multiply throughput by 5–10×.
🔒 Privacy & GDPR Medium Impact

Live camera streams raise privacy concerns. The system currently stores no personal data, but regulatory compliance varies by region and deployment context.

Mitigation Apply on-device face blurring, provide visible camera notices, and consult local data-protection regulations.
📡 Single-Camera View Low Impact

One camera covers a limited field of view. Large or irregularly shaped venues with blind spots will have inaccurate total occupancy readings.

Mitigation Multi-camera fusion support is on the roadmap for Q2.
🐢 No Persistent Storage Low Impact

Occupancy history is stored in-memory and lost on server restart. Long-term analytics and trend reports are not possible without a database layer.

Mitigation A SQLite or InfluxDB backend integration is planned to persist time-series occupancy data.
